What is On-Page SEO?

On-page SEO means optimizing elements inside each webpage so search engines can understand the content and rank it appropriately for relevant searches.

On-Page SEO Checklist

  • Use a descriptive, keyword-rich title tag
  • Write a compelling meta description (under 160 characters)
  • Use one H1 heading per page
  • Use H2 and H3 for subheadings
  • Place your target keyword naturally in the content
  • Add internal links to related articles
  • Optimize images with descriptive alt text

Title Tag Optimization

Your title tag is one of the most important on-page SEO factors. It should contain your main keyword and accurately describe the page content. Keep it between 50–60 characters so it displays fully in search results.

Heading Structure

Use only one H1 heading per page — this is your main topic. Use H2 for major sections and H3 for sub-sections. A clear heading hierarchy helps both readers and search engine crawlers understand your content structure.

Internal Linking

Link related articles together throughout your content. Internal links help readers discover more of your content and help search engines understand the relationships between your pages.
