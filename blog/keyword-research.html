What is Keyword Research?

Keyword research means finding the exact words and phrases people type into Google. When you know what your audience searches for, you can create content that directly answers their questions — and ranks for those searches.

Why Keyword Research Matters

Without keyword research, you might write excellent content that nobody ever finds. Targeting the wrong keywords means missing the people who need your content the most.

Types of Keywords

  • Short-tail keywords — Broad, high-volume terms (e.g. "SEO")
  • Long-tail keywords — Specific phrases with lower competition (e.g. "SEO for beginners free guide")
  • Informational keywords — Questions people want answered (e.g. "what is SEO")
  • Transactional keywords — Keywords that show buying intent (e.g. "best SEO tool")

How to Do Keyword Research

  • Start by typing your topic into Google and note the autocomplete suggestions
  • Check the "People Also Ask" section in search results
  • Look at "Related searches" at the bottom of the results page
  • Use free keyword tools to get data on search volume
  • Prioritize low-competition keywords if you're just starting out

Free Keyword Tools

  • Google Search — Autocomplete and related searches
  • Ubersuggest — Free keyword ideas and difficulty scores
  • Google Trends — See if a keyword is growing or declining
  • Google Search Console — See which keywords already bring traffic to your site
